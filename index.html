About ME

I have always been fascinated by the power of science, particularly in the field of Aerospace Engineering. Mathematics and Physics were two of my favorite subjects in school, and the sight of flight always captivated me. As a result, I pursued a degree in Mechanical Engineering, focusing on fluid mechanics, rocket spacecraft and propulsion, and wind energy. In my third year, I enrolled in a graduate-level course in Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD), where I learned about Navier-Stokes equations, finite volume methods, and pressure-velocity coupling, among other concepts.

During my undergraduate studies, I had the opportunity to work on various projects related to aerodynamics and CFD. In my second year, I simulated flow over Eppler E61 and NACA 0012 airfoils using the Immerse Boundary Projection method and an open-source solver called PetIBM. In the following year, I worked on analyzing the aerodynamics of quadcopters using ANSYS Fluent and MRF models. In the immediate next semester, I had the chance to work on two unique projects that further nurtured my knowledge in aerodynamics and CFD. My research experiences and internships at institutions such as the Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay and the Politecnico di Milano have further enhanced my knowledge and skills in the field. At the Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay, I worked on the flow modeling of a multi-rotor unmanned aerial vehicle to analyze the vortices formed by the airfoils. At the Politecnico di Milano, I had the opportunity to contribute to a project titled "CFD Analysis of High-Pressure Gas Releases from Nozzles" for a period of seven months.
